Our Thesis

Financial literacy isn't knowledge. It's behavior under pressure.

Most financial education teaches definitions — interest rates, budgets, spreadsheets. We start somewhere else: the moment someone actually has to decide. That's where poverty gets reinforced, or broken.

GCL designs programs around behavioral economics — the science of how people actually choose, not how textbooks say they should. We equip people not just to survive, but to thrive.

Built and run by young people, for the communities most locked out of financial access.
